Collections


Collection Composition
Our game collection is divided into two main categories; console and tabletop. Console will consist of any game with which a video game console is needed to play, such as a PlayStation, Xbox, or Nintendo Wii while the tabletop game collection consists of dice, card, and board games. Console games will be available for one week checkout while the tabletop games will only be available for in library use. Both types of materials will be held at the main desk and can be checked out using a library card. The library will retain one copy of available console titles, one copy of each board game, and two copies of each dice/card based game.

Collection Accessibility
All dice, card and board game titles will focus on games that can be completed in under an hour in order to maintain a good play experience for the patrons while giving the players enough time to solidly complete multiple games. The library website will have multiple links to YouTube video tutorial of the tabletop games available, as well as embedded links to game trailers of console games available at the library. This works to show the patrons the materials available and allows them to have a better understanding of the material they are checking out. The board games for example often have a multitude of rules that become intuitive after the first play through, but the accessibility of a video may make it more appealing to patrons who do not want to comb over a difficult rulebook. Preservation

To ensure that the material is well maintained the library will clean discs upon their return. Given that many of the tabletop pieces are durable plastic they do not need much upkeep, cards however can be placed into card sleeves to extend their usability relatively cheaply. In extreme cases lamination can be used to extend the life of the more fragile game components.
